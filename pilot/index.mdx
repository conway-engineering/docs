You are working with a **Pilot deployment** of Conway. A Pilot is a scoped, time-boxed version of the product configured for your organization so your team can evaluate Conway against real data without running the full platform.

Compared to a full deployment, a Pilot intentionally focuses on a narrow slice of Conway's functionality. The rest of this reference guide describes every Conway feature — not all of them will be present in your Pilot.

## What a Pilot gives you

- A working Detection hub wired to one or more of your data warehouses.
- A fixed set of automations (playbooks) preconfigured by the Conway team for your use cases.
- Investigation outputs — runs, reports, and the chat assistant — so you can see how Conway reasons about your data end to end.
- A dedicated point of contact from the Conway team for support, access changes, and incidents.

## What a Pilot does not include

See [Limitations](/pilot/limitations) for the full list. At a high level: the **Response** workflow (cases, dispositions, SLAs) and continuous **rule monitoring** are not part of a Pilot. Those features become available in a full deployment.
