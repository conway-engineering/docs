The **Determination** tab is where you record the final call on a case — the outcome that closes it out and feeds back into Conway's learning. Every case eventually ends with a determination.

<Note>
**Cases are not available in Pilot deployments.** See [Pilot limitations](/pilot/limitations).
</Note>

## Top-level dispositions

Three options:

- **TRUE POSITIVE** — the activity was of concern and action was warranted.
- **FALSE POSITIVE** — the activity turned out to be benign.
- **INCONCLUSIVE** — evidence was insufficient to decide.

Pick one of the three to reveal the subcategories.

## Subcategories

Each top-level disposition asks you for a reason:

- **True positive**: *ATO via SIM swap*, *ATO via credential stuffing*, *ATO via session hijack*, *ATO via phishing*, *Insider threat*.
- **False positive**: *Legitimate user*, *Known travel*, *VPN/proxy usage*, *Device change*.
- **Inconclusive**: *Insufficient evidence*, *Awaiting information*.

Be specific — the subcategory feeds back into Conway's models. A case closed as *True positive / ATO via phishing* teaches the system something a bare *True positive* doesn't.

## Walkthrough: closing a case

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm the checklist is done">
    Open [RFI / Checklist](/case-detail/rfi-checklist) and make sure every required item is ticked. Determination won't close a case with unresolved required items.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick the disposition">
    Click **TRUE POSITIVE**, **FALSE POSITIVE**, or **INCONCLUSIVE**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick a subcategory">
    Choose the one that best describes the case.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add justification">
    Write a short paragraph explaining the reasoning. Future analysts and auditors will read this.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    The case moves to **Resolved** on the Response queue. The determination is locked into the [audit trail](/case-detail/audit-trail) — no silent changes from here on.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Re-opening a determination

Determinations can be revisited. Re-open the case, pick a new disposition, and supply a reason — the change creates a new audit trail entry rather than overwriting the old one. The original determination stays visible alongside the new one.
