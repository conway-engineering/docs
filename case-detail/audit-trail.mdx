The **Audit Trail** tab is the case's full history — a chronological log of every state change, action, note, RFI, evidence upload, and determination change, each stamped with actor and timestamp.

<Note>
**Cases are not available in Pilot deployments.** See [Pilot limitations](/pilot/limitations).
</Note>

## The trail

Scroll the trail from top to bottom to see the case as it unfolded — who moved it from *Open* to *Investigating*, which RFIs went out, what actions were taken and when the determination was recorded. Each entry carries an actor, a timestamp, a short description, and a link back to the object involved (the RFI, the evidence, the finding). Use this tab when you're reviewing a case before closing it, handing it off, or answering an auditor's *"who did what, when?"*

## Filters

Filter the trail by **actor** (focus on one analyst or on automated system events), **event type** (status changes, actions, notes, RFIs, evidence, determinations), or **date range**. Useful when a long-running case has hundreds of entries and you only need the last 24 hours or the steps one specific analyst took.

## What can and can't change

Nothing on the audit trail is silently editable. Determinations can be re-opened and changed, notes can be added, but every such change creates a **new** entry rather than overwriting an old one. The full history is preserved — older entries stay visible alongside the new ones.
