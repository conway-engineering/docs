Clicking a case in Response opens its detail page — the workspace you use to work the case from open to close.

<Note>
**Cases are not available in Pilot deployments.** See [Pilot limitations](/pilot/limitations).
</Note>

## The case header

The top of the page tells you who and what this case is about: title, an editable summary you can tighten as you learn more, the SLA, the assigned analyst, and the severity. Use the severity control in the breadcrumb row (**Cases › &lt;id&gt;**) to override severity when your read differs from the system's. **Cases** in the breadcrumb takes you back to the queue.

## The command bar

Three quick-action buttons sit above the tabs — every one is recorded in the [audit trail](/case-detail/audit-trail).

- **Action** — take a concrete response: *Freeze Account*, *Reset Credentials*, *Block IP*, *Escalate to Tier 2*, *Flag for Review*. Opens a structured confirmation dialog before committing.
- **RFI** — send a **request for information** to someone (an analyst on your team, a customer, an internal partner). The open asks show up in the case's [RFI / Checklist tab](/case-detail/rfi-checklist).
- **Note** — drop a free-text note. Visible to everyone on the case.

## Tabs

Working a case is a path through six tabs. You won't always touch all of them, but this is the order most analysts use:

| Tab | What you do there |
| --- | --- |
| [**Evidence**](/case-detail/evidence) | Read what happened, attach documents, scrub the timeline. |
| [**Entities / Findings**](/case-detail/entities-findings) | Understand who's involved and what Conway surfaced about them. |
| [**RFI / Checklist**](/case-detail/rfi-checklist) | Send asks, work through the required steps. |
| [**Determination**](/case-detail/determination) | Record your disposition — *True positive*, *False positive*, *Inconclusive*. |
| [**Audit Trail**](/case-detail/audit-trail) | Confirm the full record of what was done before closing. |
