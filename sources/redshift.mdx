Connecting Redshift is a short form: point Conway at your cluster, give it credentials with just enough access to read the tables you care about, and test it.

## Service account safety

The user Conway authenticates as should only have the permissions it needs to read the tables you want analyzed. Avoid superusers and broadly privileged accounts — scope the account to exactly the tables in play.

## Walkthrough: connecting a new Redshift warehouse

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the dialog">
    From [Sources overview](/sources/overview), click **Add Data Source** and pick Redshift.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in cluster details">
    Name the connection and supply host, port (usually `5439`), database, schema, username, and password.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Click **Add Data Source**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test it">
    Run a [test connection](/sources/testing) before kicking off any live runs.
  </Step>
</Steps>
