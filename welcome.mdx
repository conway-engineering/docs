Conway is an AI fraud and AML agent. It runs detection over your data, produces investigations with evidence and verdicts, and hands the resulting cases to your analysts to work end to end. This guide is your reference for the Conway application — every screen, every tab, every button explained in plain English.

## Who this guide is for

Fraud analysts and other everyday users of Conway. No engineering background required. If you know what you are looking for, it is documented here; if you do not, start with the Detection → Response arc and you will end up everywhere that matters.

## How to use this guide

- Start at **[Navigating](/navigating)** if you have never opened Conway before.
- Read **[Core concepts](/core-concepts)** once — it explains the terms (run, investigation, report, entity, case, playbook) that show up everywhere.
- Use the left sidebar as a reference. Every major screen has its own page with a walkthrough and a reference list of every interactive element.
- When you get stuck in the product, the fastest answer is usually in the **[Chat assistant](/chat/overview)** — drag what you are looking at into the chat panel.

## How this guide is organized

- **Getting started** — the lay of the land: navigation, core concepts.
- **Detection** — the main hub where runs are created and investigations reviewed.
- **Run detail** — what to look at when you open a single run.
- **Response** — working cases end to end.
- **Case detail** — the single-case workspace.
- **Automations** — building and editing playbooks.
- **Sources** — connecting to your data warehouse.
- **Chat assistant** — Conway's built-in assistant.

The **Pilot** tab (top of the page) contains information specific to your deployment — what a Pilot is and is not, who to contact, which sources are preconfigured, and how your internal terminology maps to Conway's.

<Note>
If a section described in this guide is missing from your sidebar, you are likely on a Pilot deployment. See [Pilot limitations](/pilot/limitations) for what is and is not included.
</Note>
