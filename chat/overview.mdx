The **chat assistant** is a pane that sits alongside any screen in Conway. It's the fastest way to make sense of what you're looking at: drop a run, report, entity, or case into chat and ask questions in plain English.

## Where the chat lives

The chat is rendered as a **pane on the right of the screen**, split next to the page content — not a modal you open. On Detection, Response, Sources, run detail, case detail, and the playbook builder it's simply present. The split between the page and the chat is draggable, so you can widen whichever side needs room.

On the Automations landing page the chat hides by default to give the playbook list full width, and returns automatically when you leave.

## Attaching artifacts

The single most useful thing to know: **you can drag any card on the page onto the chat pane**. When you drop, a pulsing overlay reads **Drop to attach** and whatever you dropped becomes part of the conversation context.

You can drag in runs, investigations, entities, rules, candidate sets, cases, or the report you're currently reading. Dropped items show up under **Attached Artifacts** above the message input; use the **Remove** control to detach.

## Asking a question

Once something is attached, type your question. Starter prompts appear above the input — see [Context-aware suggestions](/chat/suggestions) for what changes when, and when to ignore them in favour of writing your own.

Answers include **citations** back into the artifact. Click a cited phrase to jump straight to the relevant investigation, entity, or telemetry entry — no separate search needed.

## What the assistant does and doesn't do

The chat will answer questions about anything attached (or anything the agent has investigated in the past), pull in intelligence and prior investigations to inform its answer, and jump you to the right place in the UI via citations.

It will **not** launch runs automatically — except through the **Start a run** suggestion, which opens the draft-run dialog for you — and it will not modify cases, playbooks, or sources on its own. Chat is read-mostly; state changes happen in the UI.

## Good things to ask

- *"Summarise this run — what's the verdict and what drove it?"* (drop a run first)
- *"Has this account come up before?"* (drop an entity)
- *"Cross-reference this investigation against the account's history."*
- *"I'm about to override this verdict — give me a second opinion first."*
- *"Which investigations would this candidate rule have flagged?"* (drop a rule)
