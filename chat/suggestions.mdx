title: 'Context-aware suggestions'
description: 'Suggestion pills that reflect the page you are on'
---

Above the chat input, a row of **suggestion pills** offers starter prompts picked from the page you're on and whatever you've attached. The heading above them reads **Try these**. Use a suggestion when you want a quick starting point rather than writing a prompt from scratch — you can always edit the prompt before sending.

## Suggestions by page

Suggestions change to match the context. A few representative examples:

- **On the Runs tab** — *Drop a run here* (ask about results, anomalies, findings), *Start a run* (fills in a draft-run prompt and opens the dialog).
- **On the Reports tab** — *Drop a report here*, *Dig deeper* (ask about evidence or methodology), *Rule match* (which rules would have flagged this?), *Search reports* (deep search across all investigations).
- **On the Intel tab** — *Drop entities here*, *Add a note* (save intelligence on an entity), *Find source reports* (which investigations mention this entity?).
- **On a rules context** — *Rules & backtesting* (ask about performance or backtests).
- **On the Response queue** — *Drop cases here*, *Assess patterns* (trends across cases).
- **On Sources** — *Evaluate usage* (compare activity and coverage across sources).
- **Default, no attachment** — *Get started* (describe what to investigate), *Recent activity*.

## When you attach something

Drop a run and the suggestions move to run-specific prompts. Drop an entity and they move to entity-specific prompts. This is why chat is most useful *after* you attach — the suggestions narrow to something actually about your artifact.

## Writing your own

Suggestions are starter prompts, not the only ones. If none of the pills match what you want to ask, type your own — specific questions get specific answers, vague questions get vague ones.
