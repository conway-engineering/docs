The chat pane is one of the ways intelligence is created and managed. An **intelligence item** is a durable observation Conway keeps for future runs and chats — see [Run detail / Intelligence](/run-detail/memories) for the underlying concept.

## Saving intelligence

Two ways:

- **Ask Conway to remember something.** Say *"Remember that this account belongs to a corporate treasurer — high-value legitimate activity is expected."* Conway confirms in the thread with an intelligence chip showing what was saved and which entity it attaches to.
- **Use the "Add a note" suggestion** on the Intel tab. Same behaviour, but with the entity already attached.

Good intelligence is **specific**, **factual**, and **attached to a concrete entity**. Broad opinions rarely help the agent.

## Finding intelligence

Intelligence shows up in three places: the **Intelligence tab** on a run (everything the run saved), the dossier of the entity the intelligence is attached to in [Intel](/detection/intel), and in chat itself when you ask *"what do we know about X?"*.

## Forgetting intelligence

Intelligence can be wrong or go stale. Remove an item by saying *"Forget what you remembered about X"* in chat (Conway confirms with a removal chip), or by opening the entity dossier or Intelligence tab and using the remove control on the card.

## The undo window

Right after a save or a forget, the chat shows an **undo** control for a short window. Click it to reverse the action. Once the window closes, one-click undo is gone — you'd need to re-save or re-forget manually.

## What makes good intelligence

Do save as intelligence:

- *"This account is a corporate treasurer; high-value daily activity is expected."*
- *"The typology ATO via SIM swap has been on the rise since Q1 2026 for our West-coast customer segment."*
- *"Merchant XYZ is a known legitimate partner; chargebacks from them are typically administrative."*

Don't save as intelligence:

- Opinions without context: *"This is fine."*
- Things that change frequently: *"Current status: green."* — today's truth is tomorrow's noise.
- Anything you'd want audited — use a case note instead.
